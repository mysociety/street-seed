There are lots of ways to improve nature and biodiversity in your area. Here are a few…

  • Working with your local councillors to add trees along your street – great for shade in the summer!
  • Linking up patches of disused space, to form green corridors between parks and green spaces.
  • Crafting bird boxes and bird feeders with your neighbours, to make your street a haven for wildlife.

It’s easy when you know how! Here’s our five steps to success…

  1. Recruit a critical mass – Community projects do best with at least 10 backers. Recruit more for guaranteed success!
  2. Get advice – Access our library of guidance and inspiration, or recruit 15 neighbours to get hands-on advice from an expert.
  3. Choose a change – Meet up with your neighbours and choose a project that suits your area.
  4. Contact the landowner – Whether it’s council land, or private land, we’ll help you make it happen.
  5. Enjoy more nature – Full steam ahead! Take action with your neighbours, and enjoy a greener, healthier neighbourhood.

Improving your area’s score is easy when you act together

And not just gardeners! It takes neighbours with all sorts of skills – maybe you can bake cakes for the group’s first meetups, or help construct fences and planters? Maybe you’re just up for spreading the word and making new friends!
